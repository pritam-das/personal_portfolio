www.eventr.ca

Imagine you are home alone on a weekend, itching to go out. However your friends are unavailable and so you end up companionless. Now what if, with a couple clicks of your mouse, you could instantaneously plan an outing with like-minded individuals with exactly the same spontaneity and interests as you? With Eventr, you could find out what are some of the events occurring around you that may strike your fancy, and plan to connect with a group of similar enthusiasts seeking an enjoyable time out.

The idea of Eventr is for you to meet and connect with other people through events around you. Eventr allows user to join/create groups inside event pages. Ideally, there would be many groups under one event. Groups will be formed based on interests. For example, I wish to go to a jazz music night at a bar. I create a group inside the event: 'Any guitarists coming in to the event?' And in the description I write: 'Want to meet up outside The Rex before going in?'. Other users, with similar interests, would join the group and chat with the group members in real time. Users also have the option to send private messages to users.

Please visit EVENTR and feel free to check out the platform for yourself!

Smoke detecting autonomous rover

The purpose of this project was to design an autonomous rover that roams around a room to continuously detect the presence of smoke. If it does, it would alert via buzzing sounds and also send warning signals to the central control unit using Wifi module. The rover’s navigation is determined by itself- it goes straight when not faced by any obstacle, and it turns 90 degrees when it senses an obstacle through the feedback from ultrasonic sensors. The microcontrollers used in this embedded system are two Arduino UNOs. One controls the obstacle avoidance, and the other is responsible for the smoke detection. The rover’s movements are made possible by rotation of two DC motors, which are connected to a H-bridge shield and which in turn is connected to the Arduino. The H-bridge shield ensures that bi-directional rotation of the motor can be achieved and the speed of the motors can also be individually controlled, enabling control to execute smooth straight movement and turnings. Detailed specifications of the project can be found here

My roles

  • Integrated the motors, H-bridge, Arduino, Ultrasonic sensor and smoke sensor into an interconnected circuit and making all the necessary electrical connections and soldering.
  • Programmed the rover in order to make it autonomous through using Arduino IDE
  • Debugged and reiterated the written program to make the machine perform the code as fluent and smoothly as possible to such that the rover is aligned straight and jerk is avoided during braking.
  • Designed the housing of the rover using basic machine shop tools.

Front doorstep Elevator for the elderly and disabled

Dr. Murray Waldman, a physician at St. John’s Rehabilitation Centre, Toronto, requested for a simple, inexpensive design that moves people with mobility issues into and out of their houses. Elderly and disabled individuals face mobility impediments while travelling in and out of their houses where stairs are the only option. Adopting existing solutions is not feasible tfor a considerable proportion of this target user group given that they are financially constrained, and some of those products require major permits to install.  The design team focused on attributes that will make the design economical, easy-to-use, and durable against all environmental conditions as well as incorporating features of portability and scalability.

Details of the final design specifications can found here.

Notable Achievements

The design team won the John W. Sender’s award for being the “Most Imaginative Design for successful application of human factors in a medical device” with a cash prize of $2000.

Programming Pleo RB: Mechatronics System and Design Integration

The course covered practical content mainly by introducing the programmers and system designers to reprogramming a toy pet dinosaur called PLEO Reborn using PAWN language, which is similar to C-programming. The course is split into 3 contests, PLEOZilla, PLEO Fetch and Emotional PLEO. Throughout the course, data from PLEO’s sensory inputs such as touch sensors, infrared sensor and binaural microphones are utilized to gain information on its surroundings and it is programmed to act according to those sensor values. Motion files are also modified as required of particular contests for desired execution of movements. The details for every contests, reports and videos can be found here.

Notable Achievements

The design team received the highest grade for both Contest 2 and 3 among the entire class due to the perfect trials. This feat was possible mainly due to the extensive debugging and testing carried out prior to the runs. From these test runs, fault or events which are previously not considered are taken into account and codes are modified based on them. PLEO is made sure to be able to complete its runs without a single hand assistance and to be as reliable as can be prior to the contests. 

My roles

  • Programmed PLEO using Pawn language to make it into a Sequential, Reactive Controlled Robot for each contest. Sensory feedbacks were utilized to make the PLEO act accordingly.
  • Edited and made new motions so that the machine response is fully aligned with the programmed instructions.
  • Debugged PLEO to extract accurate sensor values and running iterative test trials until the run for each contest was perfect
  • Came up with the best control architecture of the robot for each contest
