Front doorstep Elevator for the elderly and disabled

Multidisciplinary Capstone is a yearlong project-based course taken by final-year undergraduate Engineering students of various discipline. The objective of this course is to utilize a 500$ budget to apply Engineering principles learned over the first three years to develop a beta-prototype for from scratch. My team consisted of four members, two members including myself are from Mechanical, one Chemical and another member completing Bioengineering. Our client, Dr. Murray Waldman, is a physician at St. John’s Rehabilitation Centre, Toronto, and he has asked for a simple, inexpensive design that moves people with mobility issues into and out of their houses. Elderly and disabled individuals face mobility impediments while travelling in and out of their houses where stairs are the only option. Adopting existing solutions is not feasible to a considerable proportion of this target user group given that they are financially constrained, and some of those products require major permits to install.

In order to address this unmet need of elderly and disabled individuals, the scope of the design has been determined by generating a set of Functions, Objectives and Constraints. The design will transport users across vertical and horizontal distances by means of converting energy into mechanical translational motion. The design team focused on attributes that will make the design economical, easy-to-use, and durable against all environmental conditions as well as incorporate features of portability and scalability.

The team generated ideas using established creative idea generation methods where group members brainstormed ideas independently and then came together to combine them. The preliminary list of ideas were then cut down to five through qualitative assessments based on how cost-effective and easy-to-use the solutions were. A final design was chosen to be the modified scissor lift.

The design team then proceeded to do engineering analysis including material, economic, mechanical analysis and component, electrical simulations and pneumatic analysis on the final design, as well as took recommendations from the client and supervisor to refine the design. The team also composed manual, maintenance and bills of materials of the design. Detailed specifications and exact models of the essential components of the design were decided on and justified.

A scaled down working prototype was developed to test for the functionality of this proposed design. The prototype was made of plywood with steel connection components. Linear actuators instead of air cylinder were used due to economic constraint, and the ramps were made to be foldable and attached to the platform. Comparison between the parameters of the prototype and the real design were analyzed. The testing was done to analyze the performance in meeting the objectives of the design. The team will be working forward to improve on the design, and has considered possible improvements such as material choice and designing an even more sophisticated user interface that makes the design easier to use as can be found in the Final Design Specifications report attached.

My Roles

I was the lead mechanical designer for the group and contributed to most of the assemblies in the SolidWorks assembly with over 100 parts. My main responsibilities were as follows:

  • Ensured parts are machinable and can be easily assembled
  • Designed full machine structure of the final design and the conceptual designs
  • Updated SoilidWorks Assembly model to the ever changing constraints and feature requirements
  • Fabricated, machined and assembled the prototype using basic machine shop tools

A secondary role that came about in the latter phase of the design was that of designing the entire electrical circuitry and programming the microcontroller that best ensured it's alignment with the desired user-interface. The responsibilities were as follows:


  • Programmed Arduino UNO to develop the user interface through which the user would interact with the control panel of the design to achieve a desirable output.
  • Designed the breadboard layout consisting of the actual components in the design
  • Performed Electrical and Computer simulations to ensure functionality of the proposed system
  • Made all the electrical connections of the components to the circuitry
