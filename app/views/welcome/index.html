Front doorstep Elevator for the elderly and disabled

Dr. Murray Waldman, is a physician at St. John’s Rehabilitation Centre, Toronto, and he asked for a simple, inexpensive design that moves people with mobility issues into and out of their houses. Elderly and disabled individuals face mobility impediments while travelling in and out of their houses where stairs are the only option. Adopting existing solutions is not feasible to a considerable proportion of this target user group given that they are financially constrained, and some of those products require major permits to install.  The design team focused on attributes that will make the design economical, easy-to-use, and durable against all environmental conditions as well as incorporate features of portability and scalability.

Details of the final design specifications can found here.

My Roles

I was the lead mechanical designer for the group and contributed to most of the assemblies in the SolidWorks assembly with over 100 parts. My main responsibilities were as follows:

  • Ensured parts are machinable and can be easily assembled
  • Designed full machine structure of the final design and the conceptual designs
  • Updated SoilidWorks Assembly model to the ever changing constraints and feature requirements
  • Fabricated, machined and assembled the prototype using basic machine shop tools

A secondary role that came about in the latter phase of the design was that of designing the entire electrical circuitry and programming the microcontroller that best ensured it's alignment with the desired user-interface. The responsibilities were as follows:


  • Programmed Arduino UNO to develop the user interface through which the user would interact with the control panel of the design to achieve a desirable output.
  • Designed the breadboard layout consisting of the actual components in the design
  • Performed Electrical and Computer simulations to ensure functionality of the proposed system
  • Made all the electrical connections of the components to the circuitry
  • Client: St. John's Rehabilitation Center
  • Date: April 2014
